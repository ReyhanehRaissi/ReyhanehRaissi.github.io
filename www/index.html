I'm
Reyhaneh Raissi

Ph.D. student in Human–Computer Interaction (HCI) at ISIR Laboratory of Sorbonne Université, advised by Gilles Bailly. My doctoral research is at the intersection of HCI and cognitive science. It contributes to the understanding the impact of retroactive transfer phenomena on users’ performance when users alternate between different interfaces. I earned my master’s degree in HCI from Université Paris-Saclay and I did the master thesis at ILDA team at INRIA. I also received a master’s degree in Global e-business from the Université de Lille within the Cristal Laboratory.

Projects

Retroactive Transfer

Retroactive transfer occurs when learning a new interface influences the performance of a previously learned interface This project expelors how alternating between interfaces leads to retroactive interface.

Collaboration on Large Displays

Tabletop display can sense multiple points of input from several users simultaneously. The goal was to design multi-group tagging for several users’ collaboration which is used for different purposes such as large datasets exploration and re-organization of digital content.

Headphones Safety

Headphone that could keep the pedestrians away from vehicle crashes.
